We are excited to offer these three delicious wines as our springtime features. Complex and developed, they will pair beautifully with our Chef’s Mother’s Day Menu.

Château Tanunda

Grand Barossa Riesling $45 

One of the oldest and largest producers in South Australia, Château Tanunda has some of the oldest vines in the region. Most of these Riesling vines were planted in the 1920s. This is a classic example of “bone dry,” with citrusy flavors of lemon/lime and passion fruit. Bright and racy, this wine matches with Raw and Chilled Shellfish.

Schramsberg Blanc de Blancs

Oak Knoll District $50 

Still family owned and operated, the Trefethen’s have been in Napa Valley since 1968. This Chardonnay is ripe with flavors of pear, apple, peach and pineapple that unfold on the palate with a creamy texture. The wine is aged in oak, which creates an elegant and rich mouthfeel. This is terrific with the Organic Chicken or Lobster Thermidor.

Stonestreet Estate Vineyards Cabernet Sauvignon

Alexander Valley $69 

This is one of the boutique offerings of Kendall-Jackson. Grown in Sonoma’s most important viticultural area, the fruit comes from small vineyard blocks with 500-2,400 feet of elevation. This is a big palate and textured style with stony, dark fruit like cassis, pomegranate and blackberry. This big and chewy wine craves Tender Beef.
